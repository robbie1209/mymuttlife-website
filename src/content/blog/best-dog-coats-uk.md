## Does Your Dog Actually Need a Coat?

Not every dog needs outerwear. Here's a quick guide:

**Definitely needs a coat:**
- Small breeds (Chihuahuas, Yorkshire Terriers, Miniature Dachshunds)
- Lean, short-coated breeds (Whippets, Greyhounds, Italian Greyhounds)
- Senior dogs (reduced ability to regulate body temperature)
- Puppies (still developing their undercoat)
- Dogs that have been clipped or shaved
- Dogs with health conditions affecting circulation

**Probably fine without:**
- Double-coated breeds (Huskies, Samoyeds, Bernese Mountain Dogs)
- Working breeds with thick coats (Border Collies, German Shepherds)
- Breeds bred for cold water (Labradors, Newfoundlands)

**Signs your dog is cold:** shivering, reluctance to walk, hunched posture, lifting paws, seeking shelter, whining. If you see these, it's coat time.

## Sizing Guide: Getting the Right Fit

A poorly-fitting coat is worse than no coat â€” it restricts movement, causes chafing, and your dog will hate wearing it.

### How to Measure Your Dog

You need two measurements:

1. **Back length:** From the base of the neck (where the collar sits) to the base of the tail. Measure along the spine with your dog standing.

2. **Chest girth:** Around the widest part of the ribcage, just behind the front legs. Keep the tape snug but not tight.

### General Sizing Tips

- **When in doubt, size up.** A slightly loose coat is better than a tight one
- **Check the brand's specific size chart** â€” they vary hugely between brands
- **Barrel-chested breeds** (Bulldogs, Staffies, Pugs) often need the next size up
- **Deep-chested breeds** (Greyhounds, Whippets) often need breed-specific coats
- **Dachshunds** â€” look for dachshund-specific coats (their shape is genuinely unique)

## How to Read a Dog Food Label (Quick Guide)

**Ingredients are listed by weight** — the first ingredient is the largest by proportion.

**Watch for:**
- ✅ "Freshly prepared chicken" or "chicken meal" — you know what you're getting
- ⚠️ "Meat and animal derivatives" — could be anything, from any animal
- ⚠️ "Cereals" — which cereals? Vague labels are a red flag
- ✅ Specific percentages (e.g., "37% chicken") — transparency is good
- ❌ Added sugars, colours, or artificial preservatives — unnecessary

**"Complete" vs "Complementary":**
- **Complete:** Contains everything your dog needs. Can be fed alone.
- **Complementary:** A snack or topper. Must be fed alongside a complete food.

Always check this — some "premium" looking wet food is actually complementary (not a full meal).

### Is Expensive Dog Food Worth It?

Honestly? Up to a point.

Moving from a budget brand full of fillers (Bakers, Wagg) to a decent mid-range food (Harringtons, James Wellbeloved) makes a noticeable difference — better stools, shinier coat, more energy.

Moving from mid-range to premium (Canagan, Lily's Kitchen) is a smaller but real improvement in ingredient quality and meat content.

Moving from premium to ultra-premium (Butternut Box, Different Dog) is the law of diminishing returns — brilliant food, but the nutritional uplift is marginal compared to the cost jump.

**Our honest advice:** Spend what you can comfortably afford, and pick the best food within your budget. A £1.50/day food fed consistently is better than a £3.50/day food you can't sustain.

## Dogs with Special Dietary Needs

### Food Allergies/Intolerances
The most common allergens in dogs: **beef, dairy, chicken, wheat, egg, soya**. If you suspect a food allergy, your vet may recommend an **elimination diet** using a novel protein (venison, duck, fish) your dog hasn't eaten before.

**Best for allergies:** James Wellbeloved, Burns, Arden Grange Sensitive

### Weight Management
Overweight dogs need reduced calories, not just smaller portions. Look for "light" formulations with lower fat and added fibre to maintain satiety.

**Best for weight loss:** Burns Weight Control, tails.com (custom calorie control), James Wellbeloved Light

### Dental Health
Dry kibble provides some mechanical cleaning, but it's not a substitute for dental care. Some brands (Royal Canin Dental, Purina DentaLife) have specific dental formulations.

## FAQs

### Should I feed my dog grain-free food?

It depends. Grain-free suits dogs with genuine grain intolerances, but most dogs digest grains perfectly well. The FDA investigated a potential link between grain-free diets and dilated cardiomyopathy (DCM) in 2018-2020 — the evidence was inconclusive but worth discussing with your vet, especially for breeds predisposed to heart conditions.

### How do I switch my dog's food?

Gradually over 7-10 days. Start with 25% new food / 75% old, and shift the ratio every 2-3 days. Rushing the transition causes digestive upset — which then gets blamed on the new food.

### Is homemade dog food better?

Not unless formulated by a veterinary nutritionist. Homemade diets frequently lack essential nutrients (calcium, zinc, vitamin D). If you want fresh food, use a pre-made complete option like Butternut Box or Different Dog.

### Can I mix wet and dry food?

Absolutely. Many owners use dry as the base and add wet food as a topper for palatability and hydration. Just adjust portions so you're not overfeeding.

### How often should adults eat?

Most adult dogs do well with two meals a day (morning and evening). Some owners prefer one meal; it's largely personal preference as long as the daily calorie needs are met.

### My dog is a fussy eater. What should I try?

1. Fresh food (Butternut Box, Different Dog) — the smell alone converts most fussy dogs
2. Warm the food slightly to release aromas
3. Add a spoonful of wet food as a topper
4. Rule out medical causes with your vet
5. Stop free-feeding — put food down for 15 minutes, then remove it

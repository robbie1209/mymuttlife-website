## Why Puppy-Proofing Matters

Puppies explore the world with their mouths. If they can reach it, they'll chew it, swallow it, or at least give it a good lick. According to the RSPCA, **accidental poisoning and foreign body ingestion** are among the top emergency vet visits for puppies in the UK â€” and most are completely preventable.

The good news? A few hours of preparation can save you thousands in vet bills and a lot of heartache.

Think of it this way: if you'd baby-proof for a crawling toddler, you need to do the same for a puppy â€” except puppies are faster, more determined, and have sharper teeth.

## The First 48 Hours: A Survival Guide

### Before Puppy Arrives

1. **Do the crawl test** â€” Get on your hands and knees in every room your puppy will access. What can you see, reach, and chew from down there? That's their world
2. **Set up a safe space** â€” A crate or puppy pen in a main room gives them a secure den. Include a comfortable bed, a stuffed Kong or chew toy, a non-tip water bowl, and an old t-shirt that smells of you
3. **Decide on no-go zones** â€” Use baby gates to block rooms that aren't puppy-proofed
4. **Hide the good shoes** â€” Seriously. Do this now

### The First Night

- Expect crying. It's normal â€” they've just left their mum and siblings
- Keep the crate near your bed (you can gradually move it later)
- A warm hot water bottle wrapped in a towel mimics body warmth
- A ticking clock nearby can mimic a heartbeat
- DON'T let them cry it out for hours â€” brief comfort visits are fine

### The First Week

- **Supervise constantly** â€” If you can't watch them, they should be in their safe space
- **Redirect, don't punish** â€” Catch them chewing something they shouldn't? Swap it for an appropriate chew toy and praise
- **Establish a routine** â€” Feed, play, toilet, sleep. Repeat. Puppies thrive on predictability
- **Start toilet training immediately** â€” Take them outside after every meal, nap, and play session. Praise outdoor success; ignore indoor accidents

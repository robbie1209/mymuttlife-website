## Introduction (150 words)

Easter bank holiday weekend — four days off, a dog who's been cooped up all winter, and the UK finally starting to look like spring arrived.

Whether you're after a dramatic coastal walk, a child-friendly country estate, or just somewhere new to let the dog sprint for half an hour, we've got you covered.

This guide covers the best dog-friendly days out across the UK for Easter 2026 (Friday 3 April – Monday 7 April), with opening times, admission notes, dog rules, and what to watch for during the busiest outdoor weekend of the year.

**Quick note before you go:** Easter coincides with lambing season, blue-green algae risk in lakes, and the start of tick season. We've covered all three below — because nobody wants a bank holiday vet visit.

Let's get into it.

## 🐾 Top Dog-Friendly Days Out — By Region

### 🏔️ South West & Dorset

**Durdle Door & Lulworth Cove, Dorset**
One of the most photographed stretches of coastline in Britain — and dogs are welcome year-round on the beach.

- **Dogs:** On leads on the beach approach path; off lead welcomed on the cliffs and Jurassic Coast path
- **Parking:** Durdle Door Holiday Park car park (pay and display, gets full fast — arrive before 9am over Easter)
- **Walk:** 5.5 miles cliff path from Lulworth to Durdle Door and back — stunning views, manageable for most fit dogs
- **Watch out for:** Cliff edges if you have a dog that doesn't recall reliably near drops
- **Facilities:** Dog water points at car park. Pub (The Castle Inn, West Lulworth) welcomes dogs in the beer garden.

---

**Dartmoor National Park, Devon**
Four hundred square miles of open moorland with no access restrictions for dogs. Off the lead, proper freedom.

- **Dogs:** Off lead on open moorland — just observe the "dog under close control near livestock" rules
- **⚠️ Lambing:** April is peak lambing season. Many farms have temporary dog exclusion zones. Look for signage.
- **Best spots:** Haytor Rocks, Burrator Reservoir, Princetown Circular, Grimspound
- **Facilities:** Most Dartmoor pubs welcome dogs. The Warren House Inn near Postbridge is famously dog-friendly.
- **Tip:** Download the Dartmoor National Park app — has dog-friendly route maps pre-loaded.

---

**South West Coast Path — any section**
The longest National Trail in England. Every section welcomes dogs. Easter is a good time to tackle the quieter stretches before peak summer crowds.

- **Standout sections for Easter:** Exmoor coast (Lynton to Porlock), Pembrokeshire coast (Wales end of the trail), Cape Cornwall to Land's End
- **Dogs:** On leads near cliffs; otherwise off lead
- **Facilities:** Varies by section — download the SWCP Association's dog-friendly map

---

### 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 South East & London

**Ashdown Forest, East Sussex**
The real-life Hundred Acre Wood. Famous for the Poohsticks Bridge, but also genuinely brilliant dog walking — 6,500 acres of heathland and forest.

- **Dogs:** Off lead on the forest heathland; on leads near the Poohsticks Bridge (it gets very busy at Easter)
- **Parking:** Multiple car parks across the forest — Duddleswell Car Park, Wren's Warren, Newbridge
- **Walk options:** 2–3 mile circular routes to 8+ mile circuits
- **Easter tip:** The Poohsticks Bridge gets mobbed by families over Easter weekend. Go early morning or head to the quieter northern section of the forest.

---

**Box Hill & Headley Heath (National Trust), Surrey**
Classic Surrey Hills viewpoint. 1,000 acres of chalk grassland and woodland. Gets busy but big enough to find your own space.

- **Dogs:** Welcome on leads near the viewpoint; off lead in most woodland
- **National Trust:** Free for NT members. Non-members: pay to park (£4–7)
- **Walk:** Box Hill circular via Stepping Stones over the River Mole — 4 miles, great for dogs who like paddling
- **Café:** Fully dog-friendly outdoor seating at the NT café at the summit

---

**Hampstead Heath, London**
If you're London-based and don't want to travel far — three off-lead dog exercise areas, Parliament Hill views, and dog-friendly ponds.

- **Off-lead areas:** Three designated zones — check the City of London Corporation map
- **Easter:** Usually busy but vast enough to find space. Morning walks recommended.
- **Facilities:** Multiple dog-friendly cafés (Brew House Café, Pergola Café)

---

### 🏔️ Wales & The Borders

**Brecon Beacons National Park (Bannau Brycheiniog), South Wales**
Now rebranded as Bannau Brycheiniog — same epic scenery, slightly less confusing signage. One of the best dog-walking National Parks in the UK.

- **Dogs:** Off lead on open access land
- **Best Easter routes:** Pen y Fan from Storey Arms (4.5 miles, Wales's most popular peak — arrive very early or go Saturday to beat the bank holiday rush), Sugar Loaf circular (4 miles from Abergavenny, less crowded), Sgwd yr Eira waterfall walk (3 miles, spectacular)
- **⚠️ Lambing:** Sheep throughout the Park. Keep dogs under close control.
- **Dog-friendly pubs:** The Felin Fach Griffin (Brecon) and The Old Barn (Crickhowell) both excellent.

---

**Pembrokeshire Coast National Park, Wales**
Wales's only coastal National Park. 186 miles of coastal path, most of it dog-friendly.

- **Best Easter beach:** Barafundle Bay (tidal, restricted to dry beach outside nesting season — check Natural Resources Wales for Easter restrictions), Manorbier, Broad Haven
- **Dogs:** On leads near ground-nesting bird areas from April 1 — check Pembrokeshire Coast NP website before visiting beaches in April
- **Note:** Some beaches operate seasonal dog restrictions from April — verify before setting off

---

### 🏔️ North & Yorkshire

**Yorkshire Dales National Park**
Wharfedale, Swaledale, Dentdale — each valley offers a completely different landscape, all brilliant for dogs.

- **Dogs:** Off lead on open moorland; on leads near livestock
- **Best Easter walks:** Bolton Abbey riverside walk (3–5 miles, dog-friendly estate, café, stepping stones), Malham Cove circular (3.5 miles, dramatic limestone pavement), Aysgarth Falls (1.5 miles, waterfall + dog-friendly pub)
- **Accommodation note:** Easter weekend is busy — most dog-friendly cottages in the Dales will be booked. If you're planning a stay, you've probably already sorted it.

---

**Lake District National Park**
Probably the most-Googled dog-friendly destination in Britain. For good reason.

- **Dogs:** Off lead on fell land; on leads near farmland
- **Easter crowds:** The Lakes gets extremely busy at Easter — consider Coniston, Ullswater, or Grasmere over Windermere/Ambleside for slightly more space
- **Best for dogs:** Aira Force waterfall walk (2.5 miles, National Trust, dog-friendly café), Tarn Hows circular (2 miles, iconic, accessible), Catbells ridge walk (4 miles, one of the best short fell walks in Britain — dogs love it)
- **⚠️ Ticks:** The Lake District has a high tick population. Check your dog after every walk. See our [complete tick prevention guide](https://mymuttlife.co.uk) for product recommendations.

---

**Peak District National Park**
Closest National Park to several major cities — ideal for day trips from Manchester, Sheffield, Derby, Nottingham.

- **Dogs:** Off lead on open moorland; restrictions apply near Chatsworth (keep on leads in formal gardens)
- **Best spots:** Stanage Edge (gritstone escarpment, brilliant for fit dogs), Dovedale stepping stones (gets packed but spectacular), Kinder Scout (full day walk, 10+ miles, not for the faint-hearted)
- **Dog-friendly pubs:** Ye Olde Cheshire Cheese (Castleton), The Angler's Rest (Miller's Dale)

## 🏖️ Dog-Friendly Easter Beaches

UK beach dog rules change on April 1 each year in many locations. Some beaches that allowed dogs all winter now impose seasonal restrictions (usually on-leads or specific sections only).

**Generally dog-friendly over Easter:**

- **Camber Sands, East Sussex** — No seasonal restrictions; dogs welcome year-round
- **Holkham Beach, Norfolk** — Dogs welcome; pine forest walk access
- **Embleton Bay, Northumberland** — Dog-friendly, stunning, quiet
- **Rhossili Bay, Gower** — No restrictions April–October (dogs welcome)
- **Sandwood Bay, Sutherland, Scotland** — Remote 4-mile walk-in; absolutely no restrictions — but it's a full day commitment
- **Portobello Beach, Edinburgh** — Dogs welcome year-round

**Check before you go:** The local council or beach management website for beach-specific restrictions. PDSA's dog-friendly beach finder is a useful starting point.

## ⚠️ Easter Weekend Health Hazards for Dogs

### 1. 🐑 Lambing Season — Keep Dogs Under Control

April is the height of lambing season across the UK. A dog chasing sheep can cause the ewe to miscarry, abandon lambs, or suffer fatal stress injuries — and farmers have the legal right to shoot a dog caught worrying livestock.

**What to do:**
- Keep dogs on a short lead when near or passing fields with sheep
- If your dog picks up the scent and starts pulling — lead on, immediately
- Look for lambing signs at field gates — these are genuinely serious
- If your dog does get into a field with sheep: call it back calmly (shouting can escalate the panic), put it on lead, and leave the field

---

### 2. 🌿 Tick Season Starts Now

Easter weekend coincides with the beginning of tick season in the UK. Woodland, bracken, long grass — the environments you'll be walking through this weekend are exactly where ticks wait.

**Quick checklist:**
- Treat your dog before the walk (Bravecto, NexGard, Seresto collar etc — check our [full tick prevention guide](https://mymuttlife.co.uk))
- Check your dog thoroughly after every walk — groin, armpits, between toes, ears, around the collar
- Use an O'Tom Tick Twister if you find one — never squeeze or burn
- Check yourself too

---

### 3. 💧 Blue-Green Algae — Not Just a Summer Problem

Blue-green algae (cyanobacteria) blooms can appear in lakes and reservoirs from early spring, especially after mild winters. It's toxic to dogs — and fast-acting.

**Warning signs in water:** Bright green or blue-green foam, scum, or paint-like streaks on the surface. Smells musty or like cut grass.

**If your dog swims in affected water:** Rinse immediately with clean water. Watch for vomiting, fitting, breathing difficulties — get to an emergency vet immediately.

**UK lakes with known bloom history:** Windermere (occasional), Rutland Water, parts of the Norfolk Broads, Loch Lomond. Check the local Environment Agency or SEPA alerts before allowing your dog to swim.

---

### 4. 🥚 Easter Food Hazards (reminder)

You've probably already read our [Easter Safety Guide](https://mymuttlife.co.uk) — but in case anyone in the family needs a reminder:

- **Chocolate** — toxic. Dark chocolate and baking chocolate are the most dangerous. Signs: vomiting, muscle tremors, seizures.
- **Hot cross buns / raisins / currants** — can cause acute kidney failure in dogs. No safe dose.
- **Xylitol** (in some sugar-free Easter sweets) — extremely toxic, causes hypoglycaemia within 30 minutes.
- **Easter roast** — cooked bones are dangerous (splinter risk). Fatty trimmings cause pancreatitis.

Emergency number: **Animal Poison Line: 01202 509000** (24/7, £35 call fee but could save your dog's life)

## 🎒 Easter Walk Kit — What to Pack

**For a half-day walk (3–5 miles):**
- Collapsible water bowl + 500ml water minimum
- High-value treats (bank holiday = new environments = recall reinforcement)
- Poop bags (double up near country houses and beaches — wardens are stricter at Easter)
- Dog first aid kit basics: tick twister, wound wash, bandage
- Towel or drying coat for muddy/wet dogs
- Your dog's ID tag (legally required in England, Scotland, Wales)

**For a full day (6+ miles):**
Add: extra water (1 litre per 30kg body weight for a full day), spare lead, snacks for the dog, and some form of dog shoes if your dog has soft paws — rocky walks like Stanage Edge can cause pad abrasions

## 🐕 Breed-Specific Considerations for Easter Walks

| Breed Type | Notes for Easter Weekend |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| **Brachycephalic (French Bulldog, Pug, Bulldog)** | Avoid steep hill walks — they struggle in warmer spring temperatures. Flat coastal or riverside walks best. Carry water, watch for overheating. |
| **Giant breeds (Saint Bernard, Newfoundland, Mastiff)** | Don't overdo it on long walks — joint stress. Two shorter walks better than one 8-mile circuit. |
| **Working/high-energy (Collie, Spaniel, Vizsla, Weimaraner)** | They will love this weekend. Give them a job — retrieve games, scent work — otherwise they'll spend the long car journey home destroying something. |
| **Puppies (under 12 months)** | 5 minutes per month of age, twice a day maximum. An 8-month-old puppy: 40 minutes twice a day max. Growth plates at risk on long hikes. |
| **Elderly dogs (8+ for large, 10+ for small)** | Shorter, flatter, slower. Two or three 20-minute walks spread across the day can be better than one long one. |
| **Greyhounds / Sighthounds** | Strong prey drive — lambing season = keep on a long lead in farmland areas. Off lead only in enclosed or fully safe areas. |

## FAQ

**Q: Can my dog go on the beach this Easter?**
A: Most UK beaches that don't have restrictions welcome dogs year-round. However, many beaches introduce seasonal restrictions from April 1 — usually on specific sections. Always check with the local council before visiting.

**Q: Are National Trust properties dog-friendly at Easter?**
A: Most welcome dogs in their grounds (not inside houses) year-round. Dogs must be on leads. Easter opening times may differ — check nationaltrust.org.uk before visiting.

**Q: Is it safe to let my dog swim in lakes this Easter?**
A: Generally yes, but check for blue-green algae warnings first (Environment Agency / SEPA website). April is early in the bloom season but mild winters can cause early appearances. Always rinse your dog after any open-water swim.

**Q: My dog found a tick after our Easter walk — what do I do?**
A: Remove it with an O'Tom Tick Twister as soon as possible. Never squeeze, burn, or apply anything to the tick. Twist anti-clockwise until it releases. Check the bite site for redness over the next week. If your dog becomes lethargic or off food, see your vet — mention the tick.

**Q: Are there any National Parks where dogs aren't allowed off lead?**
A: Dogs are allowed off lead on open access land in all UK National Parks, but must be on leads near livestock. During lambing season (March–May), many farmers temporarily fence off additional areas. Always observe signage.

**Q: We're going to the Lake District for Easter — what should we know?**
A: Crowds are significant over Easter weekend. Arrive early (before 9am) for car parks at Coniston, Ullswater, and Grasmere. Tick check after every walk in bracken. Keep dogs on leads near all farmland — lambing in full swing. Water from streams is generally fine for dogs but avoid stagnant water.

## GPS Tracker vs AirTag: The Real Difference

This deserves its own section because it's the most common question we get.

**GPS trackers** (Tractive, PitPat, Pawfit) use satellite positioning + cellular data to send your dog's location to your phone. They work independently, in real time, anywhere with mobile signal.

**AirTags** use Bluetooth. They piggyback on Apple's Find My network â€” meaning they only update their location when they're within range of someone else's iPhone. In a busy shopping centre, that works. In a field in the Yorkshire Dales? You might not get an update for hours.

**Our advice:** If you can only afford an AirTag, it's better than nothing. But if you're serious about tracking your dog, invest in a proper GPS tracker. The peace of mind is worth the subscription.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do GPS dog trackers work in the UK countryside?
Yes â€” proper GPS trackers (Tractive, PitPat, Pawfit) work anywhere with mobile phone signal. In very remote areas with no signal at all, tracking may be delayed until signal returns. AirTags do NOT work reliably in rural areas.

### Can I use a GPS tracker on a cat?
Yes, but check the weight. Most dog GPS trackers are too heavy for cats. Tractive makes a specific CAT model which is smaller and lighter. PitPat also works for cats.

### Do I need a subscription?
For most GPS trackers, yes. The tracker needs a cellular data connection to send location data to your phone, and that costs money. PitPat and AirTag are the only subscription-free options in our list.

### How long does the battery last?
Most GPS trackers last 7-14 days between charges. Battery life depends on how often you use LIVE tracking (which drains battery faster) vs standard mode. The Fi collar is exceptional at up to 3 months.

### Is a GPS tracker worth it?
If your dog has ever escaped, pulled free from a lead, or you walk in areas where they could get lost â€” absolutely yes. The cost of a GPS tracker is nothing compared to the cost (emotional and financial) of a lost dog. Missing dog searches, vet bills, and the heartbreak of not knowing where they are make the Â£5/month subscription trivial.

### Can GPS trackers be removed by thieves?
Most clip or attach to a collar. A determined thief could remove it, but they'd need to catch your dog first and know it's there. Some trackers (like Fi) are integrated into the collar itself, making removal harder.

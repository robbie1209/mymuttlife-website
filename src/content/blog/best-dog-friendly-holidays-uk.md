## The 10 Best Dog-Friendly Holiday Destinations in the UK (2026)

Based on booking data from the UK's biggest holiday cottage providers, these are the destinations dog owners are flocking to this year.

### 1. The Lake District, Cumbria

**Why dogs love it:** Over 3,000 dog-friendly bookings already confirmed for 2026. Sixteen lakes, countless fell walks, and some of the most beautiful scenery in England. William Wordsworth was inspired here — your Labrador will be too.

**Best for:** Active dogs who love swimming and long walks
**Dog-friendly highlights:**
- Grizedale Forest — miles of off-lead trails through ancient woodland
- Tarn Hows — gentle circular walk with stunning views (dogs on leads near livestock)
- Numerous dog-friendly pubs, including The Drunken Duck and The Old Dungeon Ghyll

**Where to stay:**
- Holiday cottages from ~£400/week (off-peak) to £1,200+ (peak summer)
- Dog-friendly glamping pods from £80/night
- [Affiliate: Sykes Cottages Lake District dog-friendly properties]

**Top tip:** Visit April–June or September–October for fewer crowds. Many fell walks have lambing restrictions from March to May — check before you go.

### 2. Northumberland

**Why dogs love it:** Nearly 2,000 pet-friendly stays booked for 2026. Over 60 miles of dramatic coastline, most of it completely dog-friendly year-round (no seasonal beach bans). Plus Kielder Water & Forest Park — England's largest forest.

**Best for:** Dogs who love empty beaches and wide-open spaces
**Dog-friendly highlights:**
- Bamburgh Beach — regularly voted the UK's best beach, dogs welcome all year
- Hadrian's Wall Path — 84-mile trail with dogs welcome
- Holy Island of Lindisfarne — spectacular tidal island (check tide times!)
- St Oswald's Way — 97-mile coastal and countryside path

**Where to stay:**
- Coastal cottages from ~£350/week
- Farm stays from ~£300/week
- [Affiliate: Canine Cottages Northumberland collection]

**Top tip:** Northumberland is brilliantly uncrowded compared to Cornwall. If your dog is reactive or nervous around other dogs, this is ideal — you can walk for miles without seeing another soul.

### 3. Scottish Highlands & Islands

**Why dogs love it:** Vast, untamed landscapes where dogs can run free. Scotland's access laws (Right to Roam) mean you can walk almost anywhere, though dogs should be kept under control near livestock and ground-nesting birds.

**Best for:** Adventure dogs, confident off-lead dogs, owners who want remote escapes
**Dog-friendly highlights:**
- Glen Affric — "the most beautiful glen in Scotland"
- Loch Lomond — swimming, walking, boat trips (many allow dogs)
- The NC500 — Scotland's answer to Route 66, with dog-friendly stops throughout
- Scottish islands like Skye, Mull, and Lewis — largely dog-paradise

**Where to stay:**
- Remote cottages from ~£400/week
- Bothies (basic mountain shelters) — free!
- Dog-friendly B&Bs from ~£70/night
- [Affiliate: Hoseasons Scottish Highlands dog-friendly lodges]

**Top tip:** Midges can be brutal from late May to September, especially near standing water. Bring a dog-safe insect repellent and avoid dawn/dusk walks near lochs during midge season.

- Bodmin Moor — wild moorland walking with few restrictions

**Where to stay:**

**Top tip:** Many Cornish beaches have seasonal dog bans from May to September (typically 10am–6pm). Always check the specific beach rules before visiting. Head to north Cornwall for more year-round access.

## Tips for a Stress-Free Dog Holiday

### Before You Book
1. **Check the pet policy thoroughly** — some "dog-friendly" places mean "one small dog only." Look for max number, size limits, and breed restrictions.
2. **Ask about enclosed gardens** — essential if your dog isn't reliable off-lead. Sites like Canine Cottages rate properties for garden security.
3. **Check for extra charges** — per-dog supplements can add up, especially with multiple dogs.
4. **Read recent reviews** — other dog owners will flag any issues.

### During Your Stay
1. **Stick to routines** — feed and walk at normal times to reduce anxiety.
2. **Don't leave your dog alone** — many properties prohibit it, and your dog may be anxious in a new place. If you need a meal out, find a dog-friendly restaurant.
3. **Carry water** — not all walks have natural water sources, and saltwater doesn't count.
4. **Check for livestock** — put your dog on a lead anywhere near sheep, cattle, or horses. This is a legal requirement in many areas and genuinely important for safety.

### Coming Home
1. **Check for ticks** — especially after walks through long grass, bracken, or woodland.
2. **Wash everything** — sandy towels, muddy coats, and slobbery car seats.
3. **Watch for post-holiday stress** — some dogs feel unsettled after a change of routine. Extra walks and attention help.
